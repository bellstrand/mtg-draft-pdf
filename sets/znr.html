Zendikar Rising

Archetypes

Party control

Leverage the "Party" mechanic while utilizing control elements like bounce spells and removal to maintain board presence and card advantage. Splashing white for flyers or black for discard can be beneficial.

Rogues, mill

Focuses on aggressive creatures with evasion (Rogues) and utilizes mill effects to disrupt your opponent's strategy by putting their cards in the graveyard

Party aggro

Quickly overwhelm your opponent with a diverse creature base that benefits from the "Party" mechanic. Red adds burn spells for reach, while black offers sacrifice synergies.

Landfall aggro

Utilizes cheap creatures that get stronger whenever you play lands, allowing for a fast and aggressive playstyle.

Landfall midrange

Focuses on growing your creatures throughout the game by placing lands onto the battlefield. It aims to outlast your opponent with a larger board presence.

Clerics, lifegain

Revolves around playing Cleric creatures and utilizing lifegain mechanics to stay ahead in the race. Black can add discard and sacrifice elements for additional advantage.

Wizards, spells

Focuses on playing cheap Wizards and utilizing instants and sorceries to control the tempo and generate card advantage.

+1/+1 counters

Grow your creatures throughout the game by placing +1/+1 counters on them. You'll want cards that reward you for playing creatures or have abilities that place additional counters.

Warriors, Equipment

Focuses on aggressive Warrior creatures and utilizes Equipment to further boost their power and evasion. White offers additional removal and anthem effects for the team.

Kicker ramp

Prioritizes getting a lot of mana early in the game by using "kicker" mechanics on spells or creatures. This allows you to cast powerful spells or creatures that wouldn't normally be playable early on.

Mechanics

Spell Land

These unique cards offer you a choice. You can either play the land side for mana early in the game or transform it into a spell later when you have more mana available. This adds flexibility and helps ensure you can play what you need when you need it.

Party

Each "Party" card requires you to control a creature of a specific type (Cleric, Rogue, Warrior, Wizard) to gain a bonus, such as a cost reduction or a pump effect. The more creature types you have, the stronger the bonuses become.

Kicker

This optional mechanic allows you to pay an additional cost when you cast a spell or creature. Kicker effects can range from granting a temporary buff to creating a whole new effect for the card.

Landfall

This ability triggers whenever you play a land onto the battlefield. Landfall can be found on creatures, enchantments, and even some lands themselves. The effects from Landfall can vary widely, from growing your creatures to drawing cards.
